## What Even Is a Window Manager?  

Simply put, a window manager is the component of your Linux system that handles the arrangement, appearance, and interactions of your application windows. It‚Äôs the difference between a neatly organized workspace and a chaotic mess of overlapping terminal windows.  

**Three main flavors of WMs:**  
1. **Stacking WMs:** Windows overlap like in Windows or macOS.  
2. **Tiling WMs:** Windows are arranged like tiles, ensuring no wasted screen space.  
3. **Dynamic WMs:** A mix of both ‚Äì because why settle for one style?
